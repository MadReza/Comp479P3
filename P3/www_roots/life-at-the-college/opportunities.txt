                End Poverty Now (EPN), a Canadian international NGO dedicated to alleviating poverty around the world, is currently looking for students interested in starting up a chapter at Concordia. Be involved with the Concordia community and clubs, raise funds, and attend chapter meetings. Those are interested will be invited to attend the Montreal team's bi-weekly meeting to learn more. Contact Elias at socialmedia@endpovertynow.ca for info.Welcome Hall Mission (4755 Acorn (door E) in St. Henri) is looking for volunteers on Dec. 15, and Dec. 19 to Dec. 22 (8:30am to 12pm) to offer customer service at their second-hand store. They are also looking for regular volunteers for the food bank, serving hot meals in the evenings, and customer service at the second-hand store after January 3rd. Volunteers must be over 18 years old. For inquiries, please benevole@missionba.com.Les Amis du Parc Meadowbrook, a local organization dedicated to the protection of natural spaces, is looking for an English-to-French volunteer translator for occasional work on web posts, letters and articles. In addition to translation experience, the volunteer will learn about issues facing green spaces in Montreal. This opportunity would be a good introduction to local activists. Contact Louise at lesamisdemeadowbrook@gmail.com for details.The Hypothermic Race (Feb. 12th 2017, at Parc Jean Drapeau) is organized by the Running Room to benefit the Tristan WilliaMS Foundation. The organizers require over 100 volunteers to help with race kit pick up, water stations, finish line and coordinating the food area. No experience necessary. Volunteers must arrive for 6:30am - the event will be complete by 1pm. Sign up here or email Ashley (ashley.tillotson@hotmail.com) for info.Join Ometz’s team of academic mentors and make a difference in the lives of vulnerable children and youth. Working closely with professionals who offer ongoing training and support. The 1-1 academic mentorship program helps students develop their academic independence. Pairings are made according to availability, preferences, and academic strengths. No prior experience necessary. French is an asset but not a requirement. For more info please contact: Marianne Christie at 514-342-0000 ext: 3381 or email marianne.christie@ometz.caThe Jamaica Association of Montreal is looking for a fine arts student to volunteer with their Footsteps children’s activities. The footsteps children’s activities take place Saturdays, 3pm to 5pm, at 6767 Cote-des-Neiges. A volunteer is needed immediately, to commit until Dec. 17 and then from January 8 until April 8 2017. The volunteer will teach arts and crafts to a group of 6 to 9 year olds. Please contact Sue Hamilton at 438-499-8229 for details or to apply.AMCAL Family Services offers an after-school homework program (Tuesday to Thursday from 4pm to 6pm) in a French school of the city of Pointe-Claire. Volunteers are needed to help children, whose first language is not French, complete their homework and studies in a positive environment. Volunteers may choose one, two, or three of the available days. Volunteers must stay for the duration of the night(s), and must speak French. Please contact Sabrina Bouffard at sbouffard@amcal.ca or call 514-694-3161 ext 305.WUSC is a leading Canadian non-profit organization in international development, committed to building a more equitable and sustainable world. WUSC Concordia needs volunteers to help with tabling in the Hall Building mezzanine (Nov. 28 to Dec. 7, 11:30am-1:30pm) and the EV Building by the FOFA Gallery (Dec. 2, 6, and 7, 12pm-1:30pm). Volunteers will talk to students about the Student Refugee Program at Concordia. Contact Marina Polosa at marina.polosa@concordia.ca for details and to apply. The Friendship Circle of Montreal is searching for volunteers for their Winter Camp, taking place between Dec. 26 and 29 at 4585 Bourret. For the camp, volunteers are paired with individuals with special needs, to participate together in activities and daily trips. Volunteer commitment is flexible, no French required. Guidance provided. To register, apply here. Contact Mushky Paris at volunteer@friendshipcircle.ca or call 514-735-2255 #227 for details.The Dish Project at Sustainable Concordia is currently recruiting volunteers for the winter semester. The project provides reusable dishes as a free and accessible alternative to disposable items at Concordia events. Volunteer tasks include strategic planning, facilitating orders, event planning, and more.
