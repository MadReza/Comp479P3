                Residence plans Commuter plansMeal Plans are valid from August 27, 2016 to May 2, 2017.
August 27, 2016 - December 22, 2016 (Fall Semester) 

January 4, 2017 - May 2 2017 (Winter Semester).

Important: Meal Plan Services are not provided December 23, 2016 through January 3, 2017. 
 The All-U-Care-to-Eat Meal Plan and Dining Dollars Plan are mandatory Plans for students living in Residence.
Please see About our Residence Plans for more information about the Plans.There is an All-U-Care-to-Eat Dining Hall on each campus for use of the Meal Plan if you have chosen the Semester Plan, otherwise you still have access to the Buzz Dining Hall on the Loyola Campus
There are seven Food Service Outlets on campus where the Dining Dollars Plan is accepted.See Where to Eat on Campus for all Dining Hall and Food Service locations, and Hours of Operations.The All-U-Care-to-Eat Meal Plan is $3,950.00The Dining Dollars Plan is $200.00Please see About our Residence Plans for details about the mandatory Residence Plans and costs.You will be able to purchase your Residence Plans as of April 1st, 2016.Please see About our Residence Plans for more details.Once you purchase your All-U-Care-to-Eat Plan and your Dining Dollars Plan, you must bring your Student ID card to one of the Food Service offices within the Dining Halls on either campus.   The Food Service provider will activate your Student ID card to register the Plans – this only takes minutes.Once your Student ID card is activated, your card can be swiped by the cashier at the Dining Halls and Food Service OutletsYou must always have your card with you in order to use your Plans.You will be required to pay cash for your purchase at the time – no exceptions.You can return with your card and receipt (within 48 hours) and the amount will be reimbursed to you on your card.You present your card for each visit at the Dining Halls which the cashier will swipe when you enterDining Dollars are used like cash at the Food Service Outlets on either campus towards purchases of food and beverage items.   Just present your card to the cashier and your purchase amount is deducted from your Dining Dollars balance.There are two ways to check your balance:
An updated balance will appear on your receipt at the time of your purchase.
Any of our cashiers can let you know by simply swiping your card without a purchase required.

You can always add more Dining Dollars by simply uploading a preferred amount (minimum of $25) on your Student ID card at any time throughout the year.   Just see the Food Service Supplier if you’d like to add more Dollars.Any student who has a balance remaining and is returning to live in Residence the following year will be permitted to carry over their balance of Dining Dollars and add it to their new Dining Dollar Plan Balance for the next Academic year.If you are not returning to live in Residence for the following Academic year, remaining Dining Dollars are not reimbursed (no exceptions will be permitted)Both Dining Halls offer daily menus that include options that are free of common allergens/intolerances and also offer vegetarian/vegan meals.Food Services encourages individualized interaction with our chefs, Dining Hall Managers and nutritionists so that they can help you with the planning of your meals and menu.While we do take every possible precaution, students with severe food allergies or restrictions should note that we cannot guarantee the total prevention of cross-contamination in prepared foods.When preparing meals at the self-cook station, we advise that those with food allergies always use diligence when sharing food preparation surfaces or cookware with other students.No – the Plans are strictly non-transferable.  Only the owner of the Plans can benefit from the tax savings, therefore the Plans cannot be used by anyone else.All friends, family or other guests must pay for their individual meals in the Dining Halls and for items at our Food Service Outlets.Yes, but in order to be able to access the Grey Nuns Dining Hall, you must accompany your guests during their visit as admittance to the Dining Hall area within the Grey Nuns building is restricted.  As stated above, the Plans are non-transferable, and all guests must pay for their meals via cash, debit or credit card.Immediately report any lost or stolen cards to the Food Service Supplier so that the Plans are temporarily deactivated to protect your fundsIf you can’t locate your card, go to the Birks Student Service Centre to begin the process to obtain a replacement.Until you receive your new card:
You will be required to provide your Student ID number to obtain your meals in the Dining Halls
You will be required to pay cash for food/beverage items purchased where Dining Dollars are normally used. (Keep your receipts)

Once you get the replacement card:
The Food Service Supplier will activate your new card so that you can begin using the card immediately.
Your Dining Dollars will be reimbursed in the amount that you’ve spent only if you are able to provide all receipts.   

The Meal Plan will begin in August 27th, 2016 and menus will be posted online and onsite at the Dining Halls at that time.   The weekly menus will rotate on a five week basis and will be posted so that you can plan your meals accordingly. Commuter Plans are valid on a per semester basis.There is an all-you-care-to-eat Dining Hall on each campus where you may use your Meal Plan.
There are seven Food Service Outlets on campus where the Dining Dollars Plan is accepted.See Where to Eat on Campus for all Dining Hall and Food Service locations, and Hours of Operations.You can purchase a Commuter Plan online or in person at one of the Food Service offices within the Dining Halls on either campus.Once you purchase your Commuter Plan, you must bring your Student ID card to one of the Food Service offices within the Dining Halls on either campus.   The Food Service provider will activate your Student ID card to register the Commuter Plan – this only takes minutes.Once your Student ID card is activated, it will serve as your Commuter Plan card.Your card will work like a debit card and the purchase amount will be deducted from your balance for purchases made where Commuter Plans are acceptedSince your Commuter Plan is tax free, all purchases are also tax-free with the exception of confectionary items such as: chocolate, candy, chips, ice cream, soft drinks, etc.  (To be confirmed)You must always have your card with you in order to use your Commuter Plan.You will be required to pay cash for your purchase at the time – no exceptions.You can return with your card and receipt (within 48 hours) and the amount will be reimbursed to you on your card.As mentioned above, each time you buy something with your Commuter Plan, the balance is deducted by the price of the item.   There are two ways to check your balance:
An updated balance will appear on your receipt at the time of your purchase.
Any of our cashiers can let you know by simply swiping your card without a purchase required.

You can always add more money by simply uploading a preferred amount (minimum of $25) on your Commuter Plan card at any time throughout the semester.  All Commuter Plans expire at the end of each semester.  Any funds left over will not be reimbursed or carried over.Both Dining Halls offer daily menus that include options that are free of common allergens/intolerances and also offer vegetarian/vegan meals.Food Services encourages individualized interaction with our chefs, Dining Hall Managers and nutritionists so that they can help you with the planning of your meals and menu.While we do take every possible precaution, students with severe food allergies or restrictions should note that we cannot guarantee the total prevention of cross-contamination in prepared foods.The use of a self-serve kitchen is included in the Meal Plan, but we advise that those with food allergies always use diligence when sharing food preparation surfaces or cookware with other students.No – Commuter Plans are strictly non-transferable.  Only the Commuter Plan owner can benefit from the tax savings, therefore the plans cannot be used by anyone else.All friends, family or other guests must pay for their individual meals in the Dining Halls and for items at our Food Outlets.Yes, but in order to be able to access the Grey Nuns Dining Hall, you must accompany your guests during their visit as admittance to the Dining Hall area within the Grey Nuns building is restricted.  As stated above, Commuter Plans are non-transferable, and all guests must pay for their meals via cash, debit or credit card.Immediately report any lost or stolen cards to the Food Service Provider so that the Commuter Plan value is temporarily deactivated to protect your fundsIf you can’t locate your card, go to the Birks Student Service Centre to begin the process to obtain a replacement.Until you receive your new card:
You will be required to pay cash for food/beverage purchases made until you receive your new card. (Keep your receipts)

Once you get the replacement card:
The Food Service Provider will activate your new card so that you can begin using the card immediately.
Your purchases will be reimbursed in the amount that you’ve spent only if you are able to provide all receipts.
