Does your supervisor make a point of highlighting health and safety measures? Do you know of a committee on campus with a safety pledge? How about a colleague who champions the importance of laboratory safety, or who has helped do away with unsound work practices?If you answered yes to any of these questions, Environmental Health and Safety (EHS) wants to hear from you.To celebrate individuals and groups that promote safe work practices at Concordia, EHS is inaugurating Concordia’s first annual Safety Recognition Awards. The awards will spotlight initiatives that improve, inspire or maintain best practices at the university.Nominees may highlight:Two awards will be presented this year: one to a safety committee, and one to a safety ambassador.This award is presented to a group within a department, unit or faculty. It recognizes a significant contribution to the improvement of health and safety in the committee’s area, or across the university.The award includes a $500 prize that will go towards a safety initiative.This award is presented to a student or a faculty/staff member in recognition of a significant contribution to the improvement of health and safety within their department or faculty, or across the university. This may include integrating health and safety practices into teaching or research activities.The award consists of an inscribed plaque and a $300 prize that will help fund a safety initiative.Nominations for the Safety Recognition Awards are now being accepted. Please submit your entry online by May 9, 2014.Learn more about Concordia’s Safety Recognition Awards.
