                ResearchersExpert commentatorsStudentsAlumniUniversity affairsSeveral media report yesterday's announcement of a five-year, $2.1-million grant to a group of Concordia researchers to support their work on bolstering the safety of Hydro Quebec's smart power grid from cyber attacks. The team is led by Mourad Debbabi, professor at the Concordia Institute for Information Systems Engineering (CIISE), who has been named principal chairholder of the Senior Industrial Research Chair in Smart Grid Security, Detection, Prevention, Mitigation and Recovery from Cyber-Physical Attacks: Montreal Gazette and the Gazette e-edition (Debbabi quoted at length), Le Devoir, Latest Canada, AMEQ en ligne (behind a paywall). The grant announcement is also covered in a news release picked up by Public.Ericsson publishes an article on its research blog written by Mourad Debbabi, professor at the Concordia Institute for Information Systems Engineering (CIISE) and associate dean, Research and Graduate Studies, in the Faculty of Engineering and Computer Science. Debbabi writes about IT security the work being done at the CIISE to train people for data security as cloud computing continues to explode and new networks come on line.Coverage continues of new Concordia research on student performance, led by Erin Barker, assistant professor of psychology in the Faculty of Arts and Science and member of the Centre for Research in Human Development (CRDH). The study, published in Developmental Psychology, describes how occasional negative moods, notably from stress around exam time, can positively impact students' grades. Co-authors of the study include psychology professor Carsten Wrosch, also with the CRDH: Les affaires, Science Daily, Medical Daily.Please note: Erin Barker will be a guest on CJAD's Natasha Hall Show, this afternoon at 2:20, to discuss her research on coping with stress over the holidays.Research by psychology professor Jim Pfaus on the effects of naturally produced dopamine is mentioned in an article in Bustle about the measurable benefits of frequent sex.Qui Fait Quoi posts a brief about a recent symposium at UQAM, where Concordia's Fabio Balli, a Milieux Institute member and Individualized Program graduate, presented an application developed for children with respiratory illnesses. LaPresse.ca reprises an opinion piece from La Presse + by Jessica Roda, research associate at the Concordia Institute for Canadian Jewish Studies and postdoctoral fellow in Concordia's Centre for Ethnographic Research and Exhibition in the Aftermath of Violence (CEREV). Roda writes about the intolerance toward the Hassidim she sees in the recent  referendum result in the Montreal borough of Outremont in favour of a zoning by-law to bar the establishment of any new houses of worship in the borough. Daniel Salée, professor in the Department of Political Science and in the School of Community and Public Affairs in the Faculty of Arts and Science, is interviewed for Radio-Canada's 'Espaces Autochtones'. Salée, heard from the 4:04-mark, talks about systemic racism toward First Nations peoples. The interviewer references an open letter onRadio-Canada written by Salée last week about the decision not to prosecute Sureté du Quebec police officers accused of mistreatment of Native women in Val-d'Or:Please note: Fred Burrill, PhD candidate in the Department of History in the Faculty of Arts and Science, will be on CTV Montreal's noon news next Monday for an interview with Mutsumi Takahashi regarding gentrification in the borough of Hochelaga-Maisonneuve. Le Journal de Montréal and RDS.ca pick up coverage of Wednesday's RSEQ (Réseau du sport étudiant du Québec) student athlete awards gala in Montreal, where competitive wrestler Linda Morais, studying exercise science at Concordia, was named Quebec student-athlete of the year in an individual sport. Morais appears in a group picture with other award recipients (5th from left).Multidisciplinary street artist Miss Teri, working toward her BFA at Concordia, is featured in Cult Montreal.Money News reprises an earlier release listing John Molson School of Business student Émilie Leduc among the members of the BMO Millennial Leaders Advisory Council on advancement of women.Global News Montreal (video and text) reports on the passing of former Quebec Superior Court Justice Fraser Martin, an alumnus of Sir George Williams University (BA) who retired from the bench two years ago, died of cancer at age 77. Global's text story is picked up by WordPress Magazine. Artist Larissa Fassler (BFA, sculpture, '99) is featured in Avenue.TD Waterhouse and Shaw Connect pick up an earlier CNW release inviting media representatives to today's inauguration (10:30 a.m. in the EV Building, Room 11.725) of the five-year NSERC/Hydro-Québec Thales Senior Industrial Research Chair on Smart Grid Security. It announces that the principal chair holder will be Mourad Debbabi, associate dean of Research and Graduate Studies in the Faculty of Engineering and Computer Science and professor in the Concordia Institute for Information Systems Engineering. The Suburban, reporting on a recent mission to Israel and the West Bank led by Montreal mayor Denis Coderre, notes the presence among the mission delegates of Concordia representatives who signed agreements with Tel Aviv University, Hebrew University, Ben-Gurion University and Jordan University of Science and Technology.
