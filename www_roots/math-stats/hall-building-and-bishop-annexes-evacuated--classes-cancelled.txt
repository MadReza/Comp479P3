The gas leak that occurred late this afternoon, as a result of construction on Bishop St. between De Maisonneuve Blvd. W. and Sherbrooke, has been resolved.As a result, all buildings are now open (please see below), except the R. UPDATE (5:30 p.m.)A gas leak led to the closure and evacuation of several buildings at Concordia.  As of July 7 at 5 p.m., all activities, including classes, are cancelled in the following buildings:The R. Howard Webster Library is also closed until further notice.Please note that the shuttle bus stop (normally situated in front of the Hall Bldg.) has been temporarily relocated to the corner of Ste-Catherine St. W. and Mackay.
 On July 7, 2016 at 4:10 p.m., Concordia’s Henry F. Hall Building and Annex Buildings (on Bishop St. between De Maisonneuve Blvd. W. and Sherbrooke St.) were evacuated due to a gas leak on a nearby construction site on Bishop Street. Classes for the remainder of the day (July 7), in these affected buildings have been cancelled.
