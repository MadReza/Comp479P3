http://www.concordia.ca/content/concordia/en/alumni-friends/applause/awards/alumni-award-forexcellenceinteaching/james-conklin.htmlJames Conklin is an assistant professor in Concordia's Department of Applied Human Sciences, where he teaches graduate courses in the Human Systems Intervention and undergraduate courses in group dynamics. James is also an associate scientist with the Bruyère Research Institute in Ottawa, a member scholar of the International Institute for Qualitative Methodology, and a fellow of the Society for Technical Communication.His research interest lies in how knowledge is created and moves within and between bounded groups in workplace settings, and how this movement affects efforts to bring about planned change.James holds a BA from the University of Manitoba, a bachelor of independent studies degree from the University of Waterloo and an MA from York University.
