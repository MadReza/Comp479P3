A post-graduation award, it funds a two-year Masters degree of the student’s choice at the University of Oxford in England.The award was originally endowed in 1903 to develop outstanding leaders in public duty ‘for the world’s future’ who can promote international understanding and peace, and continues to offer this life-changing study opportunity to those who not only excel academically, but also in character, leadership and commitment to service in their own community and wider.Concordia’s three most recent Rhodes Scholars were Michael Noonan (2012, Dept. of Biology), Liliane Chamas (2009, Dept. of Biology and Science College) and Carol McQueen (1995, Dept. of History and Liberal Arts College).Interested in applying? The submissions page for applications is now open for 2016 and will close Friday October 14th at midnight.
