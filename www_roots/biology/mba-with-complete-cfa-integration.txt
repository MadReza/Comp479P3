By combining the CFA credential with an MBA focused on a comprehensive business education, candidates are setting themselves up for highly successful careers as corporate leaders. We need scholars and financial professionals with strong academic backgrounds to investigate the realities of investor psychology and financial markets in order to better assist clients achieve their investment objectives.The MBA with Complete CFA Integration provides you with an opportunity to pursue two world-recognized designations simultaneously. Courses are offered in Toronto and Montreal, giving you the benefits of a full-time program with the flexibility of a part-time schedule. This structure allows 70 per cent of our cohort to work while they study, allowing them to continue to accumulate the four years of work experience required to earn the CFA charter.Our program is partnered with the CFA Institute, enabling you to take advantage of the latest material the global association has to offer. Along with faculty members that are at the forefront of academic research and investment management practices, our curriculum ensures that students have pass rates that often double the worldwide average.Requirements for the Degree and Program ProceduresTo earn the Master of Business Administration (Investment Management option) degree from Concordia University, the student must successfully complete 57 credits and must pass Level I of the CFA® exams. Students are expected to complete the degree in no more than five years from the year of initial registration in the program.Please consult the graduate calendar for complete details.Note: there is no minimum work experience requirementPlease review the department admission requirements and read the how-to guide for general application procedures.Courses typically offered include:Consult our department site for a complete course listing.
 Our MBA program is the only one in the world that completely integrates the CFA Body of Knowledge™ into its curriculum. This forms the basis of the three CFA exams that need to be passed in order to obtain the CFA charter.MBA Year 1/CFA Level I - focuses on investment valuation and portfolio management tools and concepts, and provides an overview of asset valuation and portfolio management processes.MBA Year 2/CFA Level II - focuses on asset valuation. You will apply the tools and concepts emphasized in Year I to analyze and value investments.MBA Year 3/CFA Level III - further explores the discipline of portfolio management. You will demonstrate a working knowledge of the portfolio management process, applying the concepts learned in Years I and II.Toronto and Montreal have recently been named in the Globe and Mail, Huffington Post and other media as the two best cities in which to live. Classes are held concurrently in both cities using a combination of traditional classroom instruction and advanced videoconferencing technology.Our Toronto facility is located on Bay Street in the heart of the city’s financial district. Courses take place on a corporate floor where all major banks rent space for their meetings, allowing students the opportunity to network with experts in the financial services industry.Students in Montreal can take advantage of state-of-the-art classrooms and facilities located in the John Molson Building. A fully equipped trading room, 36 private study spaces and 22 conference rooms are just some of the features of this LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) silver-certified landmark.The Goodman Institute of Investment Management provides additional study assistance with four days of intensive courses just before each level of the CFA examinations. These courses are designed to prepare students for CFA exam writing and are taught by CFA instructors. Courses are available at the end of April or early May in both Toronto and Montreal, and tuition fees cover the costs of the preparation week.Our program is also enriched with specialized activities, including interview skills training sessions, career networking services, exam writing and study skills workshops, and a business leaders speaker series.A number of fellowships, awards and bursaries are offered in recognition of academic excellence and on the basis of financial need, ranging from $2,500 to $18,000 per year.The Ned Goodman Teaching Assistantship Endowment, valued at $4,750, is open primarily to current Year 2 students preparing to enter Year 3 of the program. Two teaching assistants are selected each academic year, with one located in Toronto and another in Montreal.Our graduates occupy various positions in all the five major banks in Canada, on Wall Street and in various specialized boutique firms. Sectors in which our alumni have found great success include asset management, investment banking, investment management, institutional sales and trading, hedge funds, private equity, research and corporate finance, and venture capital.Hear directly from our graduates as they discuss how the MBA with Complete CFA Integration helped them become experts in their fields.Email: Marie.Lyster@concordia.caTel.: 514-848-2424 ext. 2728Location:
