A Pap test is used to screen for precancerous conditions of the cervix and cervical cancer.Sexually active women who are 21 years old and older should have a Pap test every 1 to 3 years, depending on your risk factors. You can have your Pap test at Health Services, as part of a complete checkup, or during one of our Pap-only clinics.  Call us to book your appointment.When you book your appointment, plan for the appointment to coincide with mid-cycle (10–20 days after the first day of your menstrual period).How to prepare for your Pap testAll these behaviours can interfere with how effective the test is, and they can hide abnormal cells.To learn more about the Pap test:    My First Pelvic Exam (Pap Test) video from The Society of Obstetricians and Gynaecologists of Canada (SOGC).Information on the Pap test.Overview of Pap test from the Canadian Cancer Society including information on abnormal Pap test results.Information on the colposcopy (this test may be prescribed by your doctor following an abnormal PAP test result).A funny and accurate article in the student paper The Concordian about pap test.
