Welcome to your complete pre-departure guide. Download our Pre-Departure Guide: English (2016-2017) | Mandarin (only last year's version is currently available. You can always consult English version for the latest updates).Keep on track: Download our checklist 
Apply for your visa and immigration documents (CAQ and Study Permit) required to study and stay in Canada.Our Pre-Arrival 101 webinars will give you an introduction to your application for immigration documents, your compulsory international health insurance plan, course registration and much more.The requirements for advising prior to registration vary according to your program of study. Refer to your Offer of Admission to find out details about course registration. Academic advising is also detailed under Step 3 in the Guide To First Year. A complete step by step online registration guide is also available. If you are required to meet with an Academic Advisor, you will not be able to register for your courses until you complete this requirements.
Contact your department for registration information.It is a good idea to arrive at least two (2) weeks before the beginning of the term. Check the Important Academic Dates and plan your travel accordingly. Remember that adjusting to a different environment and culture in a country you are unaccustomed to can be both challenging and rewarding.  It is important to prepare yourself for a smooth transition to help out with adapting to your new life. Consider your housing options. Make arrangements for temporary accommodation during your initial stay in Canada. Refer to the HoJo housing and job bank or Tourism Montréal for temporary accommodation. Pre-Arrival 102 webinar series will give you some tips on smart packing and take you through your journey to Montreal, including going through the customs. Each webinar also talks about what to expect upon your arrival and your to-do list as an international student during first two weeks of your life in Montreal.
